“There are times when we must speak, not because you are going to change the other person, but because if you don’t speak, they have changed you.” - Cleo Wade

Amidst our nation’s turbulent times, we are led by those who, through powerful activism, inspire hope for a just and racially equal society. Unfortunately, their words and actions at times are met with a deafening silence. Many remain silent for a multitude of reasons, such as not knowing what to say, citizenship status/undocumentation, or fear of retaliation and digital scrutiny.

We believe in encouraging, educating, and empathizing with those afraid to speak up. Dear Deafening Silence, serves as both a podium and a safe haven. Not only do we broadcast your powerful video letters inspiring courage and vulnerability, but we also provide a solution for those who wish to remain anonymous while their sentiments are still heard. Please join our movement by following the below guidelines:

Create a Video Letter

  1. Record a video letter starting with “Dear Deafening Silence,” and address the message on your mind.
  2. End the video saying “Sincerely, Those Who Listen”
  3. DM #deardeafeningsilence with your video submission
  4. If you post on your own socials, please tag @deardeafeningsilence and use #deardeafeningsilence

Remain Anonymous

  1. Write a letter starting with “Dear Deafening Silence,” then your message

  2. Sign the letter with “Sincerely, Those Who Listen”
  3. DM @deardeafeningsilence your written letter submission
  4. If you post on your own socials, please tag @deardeafeningsilence and use #deardeafeningsilence

Note: We highly encourage you to recite your letter on camera. While carefully considered words have staying power, there is nothing more intimate and human than showing face on social media. We’ve seen Killer Mike and Trevor Noah’s powerful words ripple through social media (among many others). We saw and heard them; their emotions raw and visible.
We need more of this.
